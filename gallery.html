Tentacle monster

Recently, I realized that I lacked knowledge in 2D animation, a skill that I believe has its importance, even at a rudimentary level. Recognizing its potential value in future career opportunities, I embarked on a journey to explore my options. After conducting some research, I made the decision to delve into bone animation, and I selected DragonBones as my software of choice. To kickstart this learning process, I created a pixel art monster and animated it with three straightforward animations. This hands-on approach allowed me to gain practical experience with the fundamentals of animation and the software tools involved.

Initially, I created a movement animation to depict how the character responds when given a movement command.

I then developed a transitional state to seamlessly switch between animation 1 and animation 3.

Lastly, I crafted a third animation to represent the character's idle state.

The effect of sound on enjoyment and preception of varying degrees of realistic environments

Research Objective:

In this study, the primary goal was to investigate the perception of realism in Unreal Engine 5.2. The researchers aimed to understand how sound affects the perception of realism and enjoyment in these environments. The focus was on exploring the subjective nature of realism and identifying the factors influencing participants' levels of immersion and enjoyment.

Methodology and Creation of Environments:

Unreal Engine was used to create three distinct environments - a forest, desert, and volcano with their landscapes being created by using the Gaea software. These environments were meticulously designed to achieve varying degrees of realism, and their features, such as landscapes, textures, and vegetation, were created using a varying combination of techniques such as: procedural content generation (PCG), procedural foliage volume (PFV), an the aforementioned Gaea. And while the sound quality remains consistent across all three levels, a specific aspect of realism is compromised. This occurs due to a reduction in asset variety within the desert map and the introduction of inconsistent elements, such as monochromatic layer-blend texturing, which is employed in the least realistic map, the volcano. Furthermore, the study introduced dynamic soundscapes, which played a significant role in enhancing realism and engagement. The use of sound, including ambient audio and sound attenuation based on location and direction, was carefully incorporated into each environment. I've discussed the creation of each environment in detail, from the material pipelines to PCG, showcasing the level of intricacy in design in the full study you can download below.

Key Findings:

The study revealed several notable findings. First, it showed that participants perceived the most familiar environment as the most realistic, emphasizing the subjective nature of realism. It was found that sound had a positive impact on realism, especially in environments that were perceived as less realistic. However, the relationship between realism and enjoyment was not straightforward, as realism alone did not guarantee enjoyment. Participants demonstrated a strong preference for variety in the environment, emphasizing that diverse features in visual, auditory, and even unrealistic physics components contributed significantly to their engagement and enjoyment.

Conclusion:

The findings underscore that to create enjoyable gaming experiences, a multifaceted approach is necessary. Realism, while important, is not a standalone determinant of enjoyment. Rather, the introduction of variety across visual, auditory, and even non-realistic physics components proves to be a key factor in engaging players and heightening their overall experience. By embracing these insights, developers can better harness the power of realism and sound to craft immersive virtual worlds that captivate and delight gamers.This is particularly relevant in today's gaming industry, where many companies chase hyper-realism. However, as supported by prior research, the relentless pursuit of ultra-realism may not always be beneficial and can even detract from the gaming experience. Instead, this study emphasizes the importance of balance, variety, and a holistic approach to create virtual worlds that resonate with players.

The questionable sufficiency of the League of Legends tutorial and the difference between strategies based on rank

Announcement:

Five months after the commencement of our study, Riot has unveiled an update focused on delivering improved assistance to new players. Darcy Ludington, Riot's technical product lead, stressed, "I played against bots for my first 17 levels and thought I was good to go," and added, "Our data demonstrates that no matter how many co-op vs. AI games a new player engaged in before entering a PvP queue, these games did not significantly enhance the new player's prospects of winning." This represents a noteworthy advancement in supporting newcomers in an innovative manner, while also validating the legitimacy of our study.

Research Goals and Objectives:

In this research paper, the central objective was to assess the effectiveness of the League of Legends (LoL) tutorial for new players and delve into possible improvements for the initial player experience. The overarching aim was to evaluate the impact of the game's introductory tutorial on new players and to investigate how supplementary information and guidance could enhance their comprehension and enjoyment of the game. Furthermore, the study sought to explore variations in player strategies according to their rank, offering insights into potential enhancements for a more enjoyable experience for newcomers.

Methodology and Data Collection:

The research approach encompassed a multifaceted investigation involving Telemetry Analysis, UX User Studies, and Survey on Enjoyability and Game Retention.

• Telemetry Analysis:This phase encompassed comprehensive data collection, involving over 500 hours of League of Legends (LoL) gameplay sourced from the LoL API. This extensive dataset enabled the analysis of crucial gameplay statistics across various player ranks. The primary objective was to identify the in-game actions that had a substantial impact on a team's likelihood of winning and to gain insights into how perceived imbalances among players of the same rank affected gameplay dynamics.

• UX User Studies:The studies employed A/B testing to assess the impact of an extended tutorial on novice players' performance and enjoyment. The tests were conducted on two groups, one receiving additional tutorial support, while the other relied solely on the game's existing tutorial. These studies also included observations of player behavior, such as their understanding of roles, objectives, and mechanics.

• Survey on Enjoyability and Game Retention:The research extended to gauging the overall enjoyability and potential game retention among new players who had played LoL for less than 10 hours. The survey was designed to evaluate the relationship between knowledge and enjoyment, shedding light on how a lack of understanding could deter players from continuing their LoL experience.

Key Findings:

•The analysis unveiled that specific in-game actions, including securing the first tower, minimizing deaths, proficient warding, and proper ward placement, significantly influenced a team's likelihood of winning. Interestingly, these crucial aspects are not adequately covered in the tutorial, with the exception of towers. Even in the case of towers, the tutorial provides only a surface-level explanation without delving into meaningful depth.

•A/B testing demonstrated that a brief additional tutorial, providing insights into objectives, ward placement, and roles, significantly improved novice players' understanding and performance. These players also expressed greater emotional investment and enjoyment of the game.

•The survey on enjoyability and game retention highlighted a strong correlation between knowledge and enjoyment. Participants who lacked knowledge about LoL's mechanics were less likely to find the game enjoyable and indicated a decreased likelihood of continuing to play.

Conclusions:

the research underscores the need for a more comprehensive and informative tutorial in LoL to enhance the new player experience and reduce toxicity. Additionally, the study emphasizes the importance of teamwork skills over individual abilities in the game. Future work may involve developing improved tutorial systems and personalized guidance to provide new players with a better understanding of their roles, item building, and game mechanics.
